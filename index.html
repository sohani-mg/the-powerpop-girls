Sabrina Carpenter, Chappell Roan and Charli XCX are dominating the pop music zeitgeist after years in the trenches of the music industry.

Their domination is particularly impressive considering veteran A-listers like Taylor Swift, Beyoncé, Ariana Grande, Dua Lipa and Billie Eilish have all released new music this year.

It can be difficult to fathom their virality or chart their successes, so we've done that for you!

Let's start with Sabrina Carpenter.

Her average monthly listenership has grown exponentially on Spotify.

Her current average monthly Spotify listenership has exceeded that of A-lister Justin Bieber.

Notably, artist Fred Again also went viral around a similar time and was nominated for Best New Artist at the Grammys.

Why wasn't Sabrina nominated? According to Billboard, she has not been entered in the best new artist competition three times, which is an automatic disqualifier.

Okay, what about your favorite artist's favorite artist, Chappell Roan?

Much like Sabrina, Chappell's rise has been meteoric.

Currently, she almost has as many average monthly listeners as Alicia Keys on Spotify.

That leaves the last "Power Pop girl", Charli XCX.

Charli's had a more tumultuous journey but "brat" has been her most successful album roll out. This fall, Charli plans to play arenas on a co-headlining tour with Troye Sivan.

So why are all these talented young women finally coming out of the woodwork now? The LA Times has speculated that the COVID-19 pandemic prevented record labels from being able to elevate young acts and this is simply “a course correction for a music industry that’s been starved for new superstars”.

Regardless of the reason, the rise of "The Power Pop Girls" shows that young women are currently thriving in the pop music industry and are paving the way for other aspiring musicians.
